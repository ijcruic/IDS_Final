Accountability: About This Data Set

This data set consists of the roll call vote faction detection results for all proposed votes on the floor of the Ukrainian Parliament. Our Research Groups has also enriched this data by adding in attributes about the Ukrainian Parliamentarians (i.e. age, method of election, profession, etc.)

Where did this data come from?

This data was taken driectly from the records on the Ukrainian Parliament's website. Attribute data about the parliamentarians were collected by researchers through translations of official parliamentary records and from searching Google and Facebook.

Who has access to it?

The data is currently proprietary, since it is still being completed and is part of a major research project. So, the data is only available for access and use from the CASOS research group.

Initial User Feedback

The ability to plot spatial positions of individuals by some shared characteristic or beahviour has always been desirable to social scientists. For this visualization I first experiemnted with 2-dimensional and 3-diemnsional plots. After showing both to some fellow researchers, who are working on the same question, we came to the conclusion that additional information is gained from 3-d dimensional plots, and so we discarded this idea and stuck with only 2 latent dimensions for representing the parliamentarians. Outside of that, fellow researchers like the ability to switch between different groupings and convocations. The only thing that was still desired that we were not able to implement was to display some kind of numerical measure of differences between different coefficient of similairites, or grouping algorithms, (like an adjusted rand index), next to the visual displays to get a better understanding of the differences between methods.
